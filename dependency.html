How It Works

Step 1: Identify

A Software Composition Analysis (SCA) pipeline scans your codebase and produces a Software Bill of Materials (SBOM)—a complete list of all open-source dependencies your application uses.

Step 2: Value

These dependencies are cross-referenced with our extracted GitHub contribution data. We calculate the development cost using the COCOMO II model to assign a tangible, monetary value.

Step 3: Understand

The result is a clear picture of how much free value you're extracting from the commons, revealing the hidden cost of free-ridership and your organization's true dependency.

Step 4: Act

Armed with this knowledge, you can make informed decisions about contributing back, supporting open-source projects, and building a sustainable relationship with the digital commons.

Now That You Know

Understanding your dependency on open-source is powerful. Most organizations don't realize the immense value they extract from the commons. Now that you do, let's explore how your organization can give back and ensure the sustainability of the projects you depend on.

Ways to Contribute

Financial Support

Many open-source projects are maintained by non-profits and depend on donations from companies and individuals. Even small contributions help sustain the projects you rely on.

Contribute Skills

Open-source projects need developers, designers, writers, and maintainers. By contributing your expertise, you directly improve the infrastructure you depend on.

Advocate & Promote

Spread awareness about the digital commons. Support policy changes that recognize and fund open-source infrastructure at local and global levels.

This playbook is meant to help you understand the commons model and act on it. Whether you are a policymaker looking for evidence-based arguments for commons investment, or a business trying to understand your relationship with open-source software, you will find tools, data, and guidance here to support your decisions. Our mission is simple: to make the case for the digital commons, and to give you everything you need to be part of building them.
